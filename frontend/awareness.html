Common Skin Myths
Myth

“Cloudy days are safe.”

UV radiation can still reach your skin even when the sky looks overcast.

Myth

“Only hot weather causes skin damage.”

Heat and UV are different. A cool day can still have dangerous UV levels.

Myth

“Young people do not need daily protection.”

UV damage builds over time, so habits formed young are important.

Facts
Fact

Skin damage can happen without immediate pain or visible redness.

Fact

Australia’s UV index is often high enough to require protection for much of the year.

Fact

Visual awareness helps people understand risk better than simple warnings alone.

Key Takeaway

By seeing the trends visually, users are more likely to understand why sun protection should become a regular habit rather than an occasional action.
