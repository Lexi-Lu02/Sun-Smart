Broad-spectrum SPF 50+
Reapply every 2 hours

Use SPF 50+ that filters both UVA and UVB on any skin you can't cover. Apply a thick layer (about 7 teaspoons for full body) 20 minutes before going out. Reapply after 2 hours and after swimming, sweating, or toweling off. Check the expiry date.

What is UPF?

UPF (Ultraviolet Protection Factor) measures how much UV a fabric blocks — like SPF for clothing. UPF covers both UVA and UVB. A white cotton T-shirt has a UPF of only about 5–7; when wet it can drop to around 3. Dense weave, dark or bright colours, and UPF-rated fabric give much better protection.

Wet or stretched clothing loses protection — use sunscreen on exposed skin and reapply after swimming or sweating.

5 things that affect protection

Colour — dark or bright absorbs more UV than white/pastels. Construction — tight weave, synthetics or denim beat thin, loose weave. UPF — labelled UPF 50+ blocks 98%. Coverage — more skin covered, more protected. Fit — looser is better; tight fabric stretches and lets more UV through.
