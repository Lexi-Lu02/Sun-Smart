Protection Tips for UV —

When the UV index is 3 or higher, protect your skin. UV is strongest between 10am and 4pm. Protection is important all year, including on cloudy and cool days—UV reflects off water, sand, cement, and snow.

🕶️
Wear sunglasses
Sunglasses protect your eyes from UV rays and reduce the risk of cataracts, and protect the skin around your eyes. Choose ones that block both UVA and UVB; wrap-around styles work best by blocking UV from the sides.
🧴
Use broad-spectrum sunscreen
Use SPF 15 or higher that filters both UVA and UVB. Apply a thick layer to all exposed skin before going outside. Reapply after 2 hours and after swimming, sweating, or toweling off. Check the expiration date.
🎩
Wear a broad-brimmed hat
Choose a hat with a brim all the way around that shades your face, ears, and the back of your neck. Tightly woven fabric (e.g. canvas) works best. Avoid straw hats with holes that let sunlight through. If you wear a baseball cap, also protect your ears and neck with clothing or sunscreen.
👕
Wear protective clothing
When possible, wear long sleeves and long pants or skirts. Tightly woven fabric offers the best protection. A wet T-shirt gives much less UV protection than a dry one; darker colors may offer more protection than light ones.
🌲
Seek shade
Stay under an umbrella, tree, or other shelter to reduce sun damage risk. Use sunscreen or protective clothing even when you’re in the shade—your best protection combines shade with other measures.
⏰
Limit exposure during peak UV
UV rays are strongest from 10am to 4pm (9am to 3pm standard time). Plan outdoor activities for early morning or late afternoon when possible, and take breaks in the shade to reapply sunscreen and rehydrate.
